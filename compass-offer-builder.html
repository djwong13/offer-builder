What's an escalation clause?
For trainingAn escalation clause automatically raises the offer above competing bids, up to a stated cap. It signals seriousness — but many listings prohibit them, so confirm the guidelines before adding one.
For trainingA seller concession is money credited to the buyer at closing (often toward closing costs). It reduces the seller's net proceeds, so listing agents evaluate your net offer — price minus concessions and any compensation asked of the seller.

05Contingencies
Inspection
Mortgage contingency
For trainingThis lets the buyer cancel if their loan falls through. Waiving it makes the offer stronger but riskier for the buyer — only advise it with strong pre-approval or cash reserves.
For trainingThe number of days the buyer has to secure a written mortgage commitment from the lender. Shorter = stronger (sellers want certainty sooner); 30–45 days is typical. Ask your lender what they can realistically hit before you commit to it.
Appraisal contingency
For trainingGap coverage is cash the buyer commits above the appraised value if the home appraises low. Offering it reassures sellers in competitive bids without fully waiving the appraisal.
Sale-of-home contingency
For trainingThis ties the purchase to the buyer selling their current home. Sellers generally prefer offers without it — and if the buyer's home is already listed, say so; it's a meaningful reassurance.
